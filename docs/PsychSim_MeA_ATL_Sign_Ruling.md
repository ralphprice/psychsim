## 1. The sign: **GABAergic. −1 is CORRECT — and correct for the WRONG REASON.**

**The MeA's principal neurons are predominantly GABAergic, and its cortical/associative output specifically
so:**
> **"The mammalian amygdala is composed of two primary functional subdivisions, classified according to
> whether the major output projection of each nucleus is excitatory or inhibitory. The posterior dorsal and
> ventral subdivisions of the medial amygdala **primarily contain inhibitory output neurons**."** (Shh/medial
> amygdala development)
> **"Most outputs from the MePD are considered inhibitory (Choi et al., 2005; Swanson & Petrovich, 1998)."**
> (J Cell Sci 2019)

**The one glutamatergic exception is specific and it does not apply here:** the **MePV**'s Lhx9 cells
originate in the ventral pallium and are glutamatergic — **but those project to the DEFENSIVE hypothalamus
(VMH), which in our model is the `MeA → VMH` edge we already built AMPA.** **The associative/cortical arm —
the one that reaches a social-semantic target like `ATL-TP` — is the GABAergic principal-neuron population.**

> **So −1 is right. But it is right because GABA-first typing order happened to match the biology, exactly
> like `VMH → vlPAG` was right by accident and `MeA → VMH` was WRONG by accident. Three fallback edges from
> the same lump, three coin-flips, and this one landed heads.** **The classification label was resting on the
> coin, not on the biology — and now it rests on the biology.**

**Ground the receptor as GABAergic. The sign does not change; its BASIS does.** *(And note the internal
consistency this buys: `MeA → VMH` AMPA/+1 for the glutamatergic MePV→defensive arm, `MeA → ATL-TP` GABA/−1
for the GABAergic principal-neuron cortical arm. The two signs are opposite AND both grounded — which is the
signature of having found the real subdivisions rather than tuned a number.)*
