# Emotional Expression — MASTER REFERENCE
### The neural architecture of expression and its regulation, for the PsychSim substrate
*Level 5 of the return-path register. Research complete; diagnostic next.*

---

## 0. Why this exists

`_DISTRESS_DISPLAY = ("CeA","PAG","BA")` — we read the **felt** state and call it the **shown** state. There
is no expression system. Consequently an agent **cannot show what it doesn't feel, nor hide what it does.**
Both are structurally forbidden, and they are the phenomena the thesis is about.

The literature turns out to be unusually good news: **expression is not a read-out in the brain either — it
is a motor system with its own anatomy, its own descending controls, and a well-mapped double dissociation
from feeling.** Nearly everything the researcher described (suppression, display rules, developmental change,
audience-dependence, individual variation) is a *consequence* of that architecture rather than something to
be modelled separately.

---

## 1. THE CORE ARCHITECTURE — two pathways to one effector

The single most important fact: **facial/vocal emotional expression is driven by two anatomically separate
descending systems that converge on the same brainstem motor nuclei.**

| | **Involuntary / Emotional pathway (IP)** | **Voluntary / Volitional pathway (VP)** |
|---|---|---|
| origin | subcortical + limbic: amygdala, hypothalamus, basal ganglia, ACC/MCC, insula, medial PFC | primary motor cortex, lateral premotor cortex |
| tract | **extrapyramidal** | **pyramidal** (corticonuclear/corticobulbar) |
| target | facial nucleus, PAG → brainstem effectors | facial nucleus directly |
| produces | spontaneous, genuine expression (the **Duchenne** smile) | posed, contrived expression (the "pyramidal smile") |
| key refs | Rinn 1984; Hopf et al. 1992; Holstege 2014; Morecraft et al. 2004 | Rinn 1984; Kuypers 1958 |

**The double dissociation is the proof, and both directions exist in patients:**
- **Volitional facial paresis** (motor-cortex/pyramidal damage): cannot smile on command, **but smiles
  symmetrically at a joke.** The limbic route is intact.
- **Emotional facial paresis / amimia** (midcingulate, insula, ACA-territory damage): **can** smile on
  command, but **not** spontaneously (Wilson 1924; Sim et al. 2005; Hopf et al. 1992).

**Two systems, either can be lost alone. The felt/shown distinction is not a modelling convenience — it is
anatomy.**

**Micro-expression leakage is the two pathways in conflict.** When a person conceals an emotion, *both*
pathways activate and compete; the involuntary one leaks through in brief fragments. Suppression is therefore
**a contest between real pathways**, not a gain applied to a display — which is exactly the researcher's
point about throttles.

## 2. THE EXPRESSION HUB — PAG and the emotional motor system

Holstege's **emotional motor system** is the descending organisation, and **PAG is its centre**:

- The PAG controls the motor systems generating **vocalization, respiration**, and coordinated emotional
  motor output. **PAG lesions cause complete mutism** in cats, monkeys and humans (Holstege & Subramanian).
- The **lateral** emotional motor system: PAG + amygdala + hypothalamus → lateral tegmentum (pons/medulla) —
  specific emotional motor actions plus **heart rate, respiration, vocalization**.
- PAG is the **integration site of affective behaviour and autonomic output** (Behbehani 1995).
- ACC, insula and OFC **instruct** PAG to generate vocalization and the exhalation that supports it
  (Jürgens 2002; Holstege 2014).
- **Crying and laughter are the paradigm cases** — generated by the emotional motor system via PAG, not by
  the speech/somatic motor system.

### 2.1 The decisive finding — PAG has TWO descending controls, and one of them is the suppressor
From the pathological laughing/crying (PLC) model, confirmed by lesion-network mapping:

> A faciorespiratory coordination centre in the **PAG** is controlled by **two pathways: an *emotional*
> system exerting *excitatory* control (descending from temporal/frontal lobes, basal ganglia, thalamus,
> hypothalamus), and a *volitional* system descending from *lateral premotor cortices that can suppress*
> laughter or crying.**

The PLC lesion network shows **positive** connectivity to cingulate/temporomesial cortex, striatum,
hypothalamus, mesencephalon, pons — and **negative** connectivity to primary motor and sensory cortices.
**Pathological laughing/crying is what happens when the volitional suppressor is lost.** Expression runs
uncontrolled — the felt state pours straight out. *That is precisely our current model's behaviour: we have
built an agent with no volitional suppressor.*

**This is the answer to "which circuits have inhibitory or excitatory effects":**
- **Excitatory onto PAG:** amygdala, hypothalamus, ACC/MCC, temporal/frontal limbic, basal ganglia.
- **Inhibitory onto PAG:** lateral premotor / motor cortex (the volitional system).
- SMA and the facial nucleus **participate in both** systems — the convergence point.

## 3. REGULATION — suppression, its locus, and its cost

Gross's process model distinguishes **antecedent-focused** regulation (situation selection/modification,
attentional deployment, **reappraisal**) from **response-focused** regulation (**expressive suppression**).
The distinction is *temporal*: reappraisal changes the emotion before it unfolds; **suppression acts on the
response after the emotional response is already underway.**

**The empirical signature of suppression — and it is exactly the felt/shown decoupling we lack:**
- Instructed suppression reduces **expressive behaviour by ~50–70%**…
- …while **subjective emotion intensity is largely unchanged**…
- …and **heart rate and skin conductance sometimes *rise*** from the effort of inhibiting.

**Suppression hides the display, does not reduce the feeling, and costs something.** Contrast reappraisal,
which reduces the feeling itself without sustained effort.

**Neural locus:** dlPFC (holds the regulation goal in working memory), vlPFC (**selects appropriate responses
and inhibits irrelevant ones**), dorsal ACC (monitors regulation success), inferior parietal (top-down
attention). Premotor regions are recruited for **both production and inhibition** of expression.

**Long-term consequences (a life-course model should produce these, not assume them):** habitual suppression
is associated with diminished emotion control, poorer interpersonal functioning, worse memory, reduced
well-being, and greater depressive symptomatology.

## 4. DISPLAY RULES — learned, audience-dependent, cultural

**Display rules are socially learned norms governing when, where and how strongly emotion is expressed.**
Critically for us, they are **acquired**, not innate:

- Taught **implicitly through modelling, correction ("Don't cry"), and reinforcement** (Malatesta & Haviland
  1982). → **These are exactly our learning pathways: modelling/imitation + consequence learning.**
- **Audience-dependent** — the canonical study is literally titled *Display Rules for Anger, Sadness, and
  Pain: It Depends on Who Is Watching* (Zeman & Garber 1996). Expression differs with **strangers vs. close
  others vs. relative status**. *This is the researcher's doctor/stranger/intimate case, already an
  established finding.*
- Strategies include **suppression, masking, and amplification** (exaggerating beyond the felt state).
- **Temperament modulates uptake**: parents socialising children identically get different responses (Brody).
- Cultural variation is large; restraint vs. openness norms differ systematically.
- Children as young as 7 can infer, from someone expressing one emotion to a partner's face and another
  behind their back, **both the expresser's true feelings and the audience's desires** — i.e. others' display
  rules are themselves read and modelled.

**Implication: display rules should not be built. They should be *learned* — by modelling, correction and
reinforcement — and expressed through the audience-sensitive social context we already have (ties, status,
proximity).**

## 5. DEVELOPMENT — the "developed intensity" the researcher asked for

The trajectory is documented and it is exactly the one described (Thompson 1991):

> the newborn **cries uncontrollably** when distressed → the toddler seeks assistance → the preschooler
> reflects and talks about feelings → the **school-age child redirects attention and can control its
> expression to others** → the adolescent uses idiosyncratic self-regulatory strategies.

Mechanistically:
- Roots in **emergent cortical inhibitory systems** regulating behavioural state.
- A transition at **~9–10 months**: frontal (dlPFC) maturation yields **response inhibition** over prepotent
  tendencies (Diamond 1988).
- **PFC undergoes protracted development across childhood and adolescence and exerts increasing control over
  emotional-reactivity regions (amygdala)** — biologically governed by frontal-striatal maturation **and
  experientially shaped by caregivers and peers**. *Both. Not either.*
- **Amygdala–mPFC functional connectivity flips from positive to negative** across the childhood→adolescence
  transition (Gee et al. 2013; Wu et al. 2016) — **a developmental sign change: the connection becomes
  inhibitory.**
- ACC–amygdala effective connectivity **increases with age** as regulation demand rises.
- Maturation of regulatory mechanisms **continues into the early twenties**.

**Individual variation is temperamental and measurable:** effortful control (inhibitory/attentional/
activation control) predicts regulation efficiency and correlates with the connectivity strength itself. Weak
self-regulators show a different mPFC–insula connectivity pattern.

**This is the answer to "operating at the intensity they have developed at":** the suppressor pathway's
strength is a **developmental fact** — set by maturation schedule × experience × temperament — never a
constant. The neurotic-vs-suppressor difference and the child-vs-adult difference are *the same parameter at
different developed strengths.*

## 6. THE PSYCHOPATHY SIGNATURE — why this is study-critical

**The expression profile in psychopathy/CU is itself a dual-pathway dissociation:**

**Involuntary pathway blunted:**
- **Shallow affect** is a hallmark — limited emotional range and depth; flat where strong feeling is expected.
- **Reduced short-latency facial mimicry** (zygomaticus/corrugator EMG) in adolescents with high CU traits
  (adult findings are mixed — a real open question).
- Reduced facial response to violent (but not comedic) material in high-CU individuals.
- Reduced **contagious yawning** — another automatic reproduction of another's state.
- The amygdala is implicated in short-latency mimicry — and amygdala hypofunction is the CU signature.

**Voluntary pathway intact or enhanced:**
- **Increased deceptive emotional expression** (Book et al. 2015; ten Brinke et al. 2017).
- They **mimic appropriate emotional responses** that lack genuine depth — *displays without feeling.*
- Manipulation and conning **require** sensitivity to social cues, in tension with the recognition deficits.
- CU traits may **dampen affiliative expressions (e.g. posed smiles) independently of underlying arousal** —
  an expression-level effect *decoupled from feeling* (Baskin-Sommers & Newman 2014).
- Psychopathic traits alter **both posed and spontaneous smiles** and the associated emotional experience.

**So: the involuntary (extrapyramidal/amygdala-driven) route is blunted while the volitional (pyramidal)
route is available and used strategically.** That *is* the dual-pathway architecture, dissociated.

**The payoff:** if we build the two pathways faithfully, **the psychopathy expression signature should
emerge** — an agent with blunted affective circuits has a weak involuntary pathway but an intact volitional
one, so it spontaneously shows little and can still pose. **We would not code "psychopaths fake emotion." It
would fall out of the anatomy.** And it is the *output* face of the reads-but-doesn't-feel dissociation that
is already our robust CU signature.

## 7. WHAT THE SUBSTRATE ALREADY HAS (to be verified in the diagnostic)

Encouraging — much of the IP is present or nearly so:
- **PAG** — present, and it is *the* hub of the emotional motor system.
- **PAG-PANIC** — present, described in-seed as *"the infant-cry/protest output"*. **The cry effector
  exists.**
- **CeA→PAG, CeA→HYPdm** — present (inhibitory; the v9 aggression keystone rides on these).
- **HYPdm, PVN, SympOut, IML, AdrenalCortex** — the autonomic/effector limb.
- **preSMA** — present, in-seed as *"motor inhibition interface (to STN)"* — a candidate volitional-inhibition
  node.
- **dlPFC, vlPFC, dmPFC, OFC, aIns** — the regulation network, largely present.
- Social context: ties, status, proximity — the audience variables display rules need.
- Learning pathways: vicarious (built) + modelling (pending) — **the exact mechanisms by which display rules
  are acquired.**

**What is missing (the shape of the gap):**
1. **No expressive output** — nothing reads the emotional motor system as a *display*; we read limbic
   circuits instead.
2. **No volitional pathway** — no motor-cortex route to expression, therefore no posing and no
   displays-without-feeling.
3. **No volitional suppressor** — no premotor→PAG inhibition. *We have effectively modelled an agent with
   pathological laughing/crying: the felt state pours straight out, unsuppressed.*
4. **No developmental trajectory of suppression** — hence no child/adult, neurotic/suppressor variation.
5. **No audience-sensitivity of display** — the social matrix exists but does not reach expression.

## 9. Standing cautions for this level
- **The two-pathway difference is structural. Do not implement it as a gain.** (The vicarious lesson: the
  reduction must come from the anatomy, not from a chosen number.)
- **Do not code display rules, suppression strength, or expressivity.** They must be *learned* and
  *developed*. If a constant appears, a mechanism is missing.
- **The psychopathy expression signature must EMERGE and be measured — never built in.** It is the study's
  subject; building it makes the model a theory.
- **Check the throttle-set overlap** before any expression claim (the construct-validity rule).
- **This level owns two re-homed branches:** the display's phasic character / chronic-distress invisibility,
  and the contagion loop's damping. Both were symptoms of having no expression system. **They close here or
  are consciously re-homed — see the return-path register.**
