## 2. ★ The grounded fix — rejection IS provocation, and the model already has the act

**The bootstrapping problem is: provocation is perceivable only from a prior aggressive act. The literature
gives the answer — the most-replicated provocation source in the aggression literature requires no prior
aggression at all:**

> **Social rejection/exclusion reliably elicits aggression.** Excluded participants behave more aggressively
> across a wide range of tasks (Twenge et al. 2001; Chow 2008; Gaertner 2008; Warburton 2006); ostracism causes
> aggression toward the source and toward innocent others; **peer rejection in childhood correlates with later
> aggression**; social rejection is named directly as "another aversive social stimulus" in the standard
> handbook treatment (Bushman & Huesmann).
> **And Berkowitz's reformulation generalises it:** aversive events "generate aggressive inclinations only to
> the extent that they produce NEGATIVE AFFECT" — including social rejection, physical pain, psychological
> discomfort. **Any negative affect can produce aggression.**

**This breaks the bootstrap directly: rejection occurs WITHOUT prior aggression.** Three grounded routes,
ordered by minimality:

### Route 1 — `separation`/rejection carries provocation (BUILD THIS)
**The `separation` act already exists, already occurs in exchanges, and currently maps only to
affiliation-negative. It should ALSO carry provocation.** This is the most-replicated finding in the literature,
it uses an act the model already has, and **it directly breaks the bootstrap — a harsh cohort member who
rejects supplies provocation without any prior aggressive act existing.** Minimal, grounded, sufficient.

### Route 2 — negative displayed affect as an aversive stimulus (BUILD THIS)
**Berkowitz: aversive events produce aggression via negative affect. P1 already put `displayed_affect` on the
exchange** — so a partner's distress/hostility display is an aversive stimulus already crossing the channel.
**It should contribute provocation.** Also minimal, also uses existing machinery, and it is the deeper grounding
(the general negative-affect route, of which rejection is one instance).

### Route 3 — environmental harshness reaching the exchange percept (REGISTER)
**The build session named this directly: "environmental harshness never reaches the exchange percept — it
colours the moral moments only."** Grounded — aggression "escalates in environments characterized by chronic
resource scarcity, unpredictable rule enforcement, or frequent goal blocking." **But it is a larger structural
change (a new pathway from the environment into the dyadic exchange), and Routes 1–2 may suffice. Register it
as the structural gap; build it only if 1–2 don't supply link 1.**
