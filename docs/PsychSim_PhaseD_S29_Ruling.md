### 1. `PMC-l` is LUMPED — the coupling is an artifact of the lump, not anatomy
`PMC-l` currently does **two** things: it **suppresses** (PLC: the lateral premotor suppresses the
faciorespiratory column) **and it plans volitional production** (premotor → M1). **One node, two functions —
so driving the suppressor necessarily drives the production.** In the real cortex those are different
populations, and a person can hold their voice without speaking. **Register `PMC-l` as a split candidate**
(fourth: `dPAG` dl/l, `dACC` cognitive/motor, `NuFac` upper/lower, now this).

### 2. `NuAmb-vocal` is lumped too — and the *display* is what cannot cope
The cry and speech genuinely share the laryngeal motoneurons — **that lumping is anatomically correct.** But
**the display reads the motoneuron pool's activation, so it cannot tell a cry from a sentence.** So *"the cry
goes up"* when `PMC-l` drives `M1-face` is **the display misreading volitional vocalisation as distress** —
a read-out limitation, exactly the `NuFac` upper/lower class. **In reality a cry and a sentence are
distinguished acoustically, not by which motoneurons fire.** Register.

### 3. ★ `M1-face` being afferent-less is CORRECT — it is a HOOK, and it is the return path arriving
**This is the real reason, and it is not a cost.**

`M1-face`'s afferent is **a learned display rule** — *"smile at the boss," "don't cry in front of
strangers."* And display rules are **learned by modelling, correction and reinforcement**, and they live in
**the representational-memory-executive layer (§2.16): beliefs, attitudes, opinions, and the rules that
control behaviour through the executive.** **That layer is D3 — the level we descended FROM. It is not
built.**

**So *"posing is a capacity with no spontaneous trigger"* is exactly the right state: the model CAN pose and
has no reason to — because the reason is learned, and the learning substrate is one level up.** Wiring a
trigger now would make posing a consequence of executive engagement — **a coded disposition to pose. That is
a coded display rule, and the standing rule forbids it.**
