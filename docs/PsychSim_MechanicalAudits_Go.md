## 1. ★ The auditory convergence — and it is sharper than either of us put it

**Your generalisation is right and it is bigger than "the subcortical auditory input doesn't exist." I
checked the whole input surface. Look at what every OTHER modality has:**

| modality | raw channel → transducer/first relay |
|---|---|
| vision | **`IN-VIS → RET`** ✅ |
| olfaction | **`IN-OLF → OB`** ✅ |
| vestibular | **`IN-VESTIB → VN`** ✅ |
| interoception | **`IN-INTERO → NTS`** ✅ |
| somatosensory | `IN-SOMATO:touch → SOM-relay` ✅ (generic, serves as raw) |
| gustation | `IN-GUST:taste → NTS-gust` ✅ (generic, serves as raw) |
| **audition** | **— NOTHING. Only `IN-AUD:voice → A1-belt`, a feature channel landing in cortex.** |

> **Audition is the ONLY modality in the seed without a raw input channel. Every other transducer has one —
> `RET`, `OB`, `VN`, `NTS`. `COCH` does not.**

**So `COCH` is not "unafferented" in the `PVN-OT`/`LC` sense — a hub nobody finished wiring. It is
unafferented because ITS CHANNEL WAS NEVER CREATED.** Different failure, and a much simpler one.

**And the fix is ONE edge, and the seed already contains its template: `IN-AUD → COCH`, the exact analogue of
`IN-VIS → RET`** (basis `anatomy`, band `moderate-strong`). **One edge, five consequences:** the subcortical
auditory chain lights · **startle can fire for the first time** · the cry reaches `SC-Pv` (which Stempel &
Evans say carries *visual **and auditory*** threat to dPAG) · the cry reaches the IC · **and door 1's
auditory half may close.**

⚠️ **But it is not free, and it needs its own clearance when it lands:** it will move the golden, and **it
will make an innate reinforcer (`PB-STARTLE`) fire for the first time in the model's existence.** That is a
lot of behaviour from one edge. **Build it as its own pass, not as a footnote to the grain.**
