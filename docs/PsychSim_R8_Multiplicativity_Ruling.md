## 1. What the literature actually says — normalisation is real, AND it must preserve relative weights

**Both halves matter and they are not in tension:**

> Homeostatic scaling is real and necessary — but it **"poses a risk of losing the distinctions among relative
> synaptic weights, which are possibly cellular correlates of memory storage."** The biological solution is that
> scaling is **MULTIPLICATIVE**: *"multiplicative scaling of all synapses… would preserve the relative weights
> among them, because they would all be proportionately adjusted"* — *"multiplicative change is a key feature
> of synaptic scaling, because it preserves information stored as different weights across synapses"*
> (Turrigiano 1998; Abbott & Nelson 2000). The total synaptic input is matched to the homeostatic range **while
> preserving the relative differences between synaptic weights.**

> **★ So the biology does NOT protect reinforced synapses by exempting them from normalisation. It normalises
> everything — proportionately — so that the LEARNED RELATIVE STRUCTURE survives the normalisation. The
> memory is preserved in the ratios, not by exemption.**

**That reframes the question entirely. It is not "should R8 protect the DA-reinforced synapse." It is:**

## 2. ★ THE DISCRIMINATING MEASUREMENT — is R8 multiplicative?

**Measure whether R8 preserves relative weights: does the cue→reward synapse's SHARE of its target's total
incoming drive survive the connectome growth, or does the share itself shrink?**

- **If R8 is multiplicative (share preserved):** the learned structure is intact; only the ABSOLUTE weight fell
  because the denominator grew. **Then this is not a plasticity defect at all — it is the DA-learning read-out
  measuring an absolute quantity where the biology stores the memory in the relative one. That would make it a
  FOURTH instance of the audit's pattern: the instrument reading the wrong quantity.** The fix would be to
  measure the associative effect on the normalised/relative weight, not to change R8.
- **If R8 is NOT multiplicative (share shrinks non-proportionally):** it is distorting relative weights — **which
  is precisely the failure mode multiplicative scaling exists in biology to prevent.** That is a genuine,
  grounded defect, and the grounded fix is to make R8 multiplicative (Turrigiano), which is a *relation* to
  ground, not a knob to tune — the same discipline as the E-I proportionality and the pacemaker rates.

**This is cheap, decisive, and it is the measurement that turns a modelling opinion into a grounded call. Take
it before ruling further.**

## 3. The framing correction — connectome growth is not a biological event

**One thing worth stating regardless of the measurement:** the erosion tracked circuit growth 78→96. **But a
real brain does not gain afferent populations because a modeller became more complete.** Those afferents were
always there in the biology; the model simply did not represent them yet.

> **So the pre-growth magnitude was inflated by an INCOMPLETE afferent set — the cue synapse held an
> artificially large share of a target that was missing most of its real inputs. The S18 law again, in its
> cleanest form: the earlier, larger effect was an artifact of incompleteness, and completing the anatomy
> deflated it toward the honest value.** That is true whether or not R8 is multiplicative, and it means at
> least part of the ~10.5× is correct deflation rather than erosion of a memory.
