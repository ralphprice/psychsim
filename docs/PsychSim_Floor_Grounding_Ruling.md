# Floor grounding — RULED. **Raise it, and the argument is stronger than capping rigidification.**

**My hypothesis, refuted by the measurement it invited. Fifth instance, and mine. The floor is inert — 0 of 86
connections reach it, 1/n lands an order of magnitude above it, so it cannot be "500× too restrictive." Owned.
But the measurement relocated the lever rather than removing it, and both decisions have clean answers.**

---

## 1. Decision (a) — raise the floor. The decisive argument is NOT "cap the rigidification."

**Here is the argument that settles it, and it is stronger than the one I made:**

> **Right now, adult plasticity level is set by an ARBITRARY HARNESS PARAMETER.** S10.1's `1/n` means the adult
> `exp_factor` ≈ 1/64 ≈ 0.016 *because the harness runs 64 episodes*. Run 128 and the adult is twice as rigid.
> The audit already flagged the same defect one layer up: episode density varies per stage (30/20/15/15 over
> the same 18 years), so "amount of development" is set by an unnormalised episode count. **The model's adult
> rigidity is currently a consequence of a modelling choice, not of a grounded quantity.**
>
> **A binding floor removes that dependence.** If the floor binds, the adult plasticity level is the floor —
> grounded to the literature — *regardless of how many episodes the harness happens to run*. **That is the real
> reason to raise it: not to cap rigidification, but to replace an arbitrary harness dependence with a grounded
> value.**

**And it correctly changes S10.1's character:** from "decay toward zero" to "decay toward a grounded
asymptote." **That is what the biology says** — plasticity remains possible throughout life, at reduced
magnitude. `1/n` governs the *approach*; the floor is the *asymptote*. Clean division of labour, both grounded.
S10.1 is not neutered; it is given the endpoint it was missing.

## 2. The VALUE — derived by measurement against the band, not set as a point

**Do not set the floor to "0.5 × developmental."** That is a units error: `1/n` reaches 0.5 at n=2, so a floor
of 0.5 would bind almost immediately and drive the adult:developmental ratio toward 1.0 — overshooting.

**Ground the RATIO; derive the value:**
1. **The grounded band:** adult plasticity is **≤50%** of the developmental magnitude in simultaneous
   juvenile-vs-adult comparisons across multiple assays. **"At most half" — a ceiling, not a point.** The model
   currently measures 0.036–0.07, roughly 7–14× too rigid.
2. **Set the floor, measure the resulting adult:developmental ratio, and land it inside the band.** One
   parameter, one grounded constraint — that is *determination from a grounded relation*, the same discipline
   as the E-I loop gains (where proportionality was grounded and the weight followed from the load). It is not
   fitting: fitting would be tuning to make a test outcome green; this calibrates a scaffold to a
   literature-grounded measurable.
3. **Report the band and where the model lands, not a false-precision point.** "Adult:developmental ratio X,
   within the literature's ≤0.5" is the honest statement. Do not claim the literature pins a specific value —
   it does not.

## 3. Decision (b) — yes, the relative/share instrument. With one guard.

**Confirmed: the co-movement test must use the relative/share quantity, not `anticipatory_value`.** The
grounding is already established: R8 is multiplicative, the memory lives in the ratios, and an absolute
read-out under a growing connectome measures the denominator. `anticipatory_value` going negative is that
confound, not a result.

**But name the guard, because switching instruments after a negative result has a fitting smell even when it is
legitimate:**

> **The legitimacy here comes from R8 being established INDEPENDENTLY and EARLIER — the instrument choice
> follows from a prior grounded finding, not from this measurement's outcome. To keep that clean:
> PRE-REGISTER the expected direction on the relative quantity BEFORE running it**, exactly as the
> emergent-findings re-verification was pre-registered. Predict what the three results should do if the floor
> is the cause, write it down, then measure. **A co-movement confirmation obtained on an instrument chosen
> after seeing a bad answer is worth much less than the same confirmation predicted in advance.**

## 4. Register separately — the unnormalised episode count

**The audit flagged it and this measurement confirms its bite: plasticity accrues per episode, episode density
varies arbitrarily per stage, and `1/n` therefore makes developmental outcome depend on a harness parameter.**
Raising the floor blunts it at the adult end, but the *developmental trajectory* still depends on episode
counts. **Register it as its own item** — the honest fix is normalising plasticity accrual to developmental
time rather than episode count, and it touches every developmental claim. Not this pass; registered.
