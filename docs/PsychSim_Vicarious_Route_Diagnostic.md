## 1. The route is live — observed distress ≈ direct punishment

phasic NA. (`IN-AUD:voice` → A1-belt.) `SC-Pv→CeA` already existed; the LC arc supplied `CeA→LC`. So observing
another's distress produces an aversive teaching signal **essentially identical to direct punishment**.

## 3. What is actually missing (→ the build is routing + gain)
- **No reduced gain.** Vicarious (0.212) ≈ direct (0.208). The research holds vicarious < direct; the substrate
  currently treats an observed distress cue as equivalent to being hurt yourself. Gain differentiation is a
  real gap.
- **The Arena presents the other's ACT, not the other's CONSEQUENCE.** `_social_episode` feeds
  `_perceive(other_last_act)`. The distress *cue* used above is the empathy probe, not something the Arena
  routes from an observed outcome. So the perceiver has the *capacity* to respond to another's distress; the
  Arena never tells it that the other was punished.

That matches the ruling's hypothesis exactly: **construction is not needed; routing (present the observed
consequence) and gain (vicarious < direct) are.**

## 4. HONESTY FLAG — the empathy-throttle implication is STRUCTURALLY ENTAILED, not emergent

Throttling affective empathy **abolishes** the vicarious NA teaching signal. The ruling anticipated the
substrate would then "be able to speak to whether low affective empathy impairs vicarious learning."

**It cannot speak to it independently, and this must not be reported as if it could.** `AFFECTIVE_EMPATHY =
(LA, BA, CeA, MeA, aIns)` — **`CeA` is IN the throttle set, and `CeA` is the sole afferent of LC.** So the
throttle hits the teaching pathway's own node. "Low affective empathy impairs vicarious aversive learning" is
**entailed by the shared node**, i.e. true *by construction of the manipulation*, not an emergent result. The
overlap is anatomically honest (CeA genuinely belongs to both the affective-empathy network and the CeA-CRF→LC
projection) — so the **structural claim** is real and worth stating: *the affective-empathy network and the
aversive-teaching pathway share CeA, so hypofunction of the former necessarily impairs the latter.* But it is a
statement about shared anatomy, **not an independent test of the hypothesis**, and it would be a serious
overclaim to present it as one. This is exactly the class of thing the punishment read-out taught us to catch
*before* it becomes a finding.
