01 The problem

Most social simulations answer their own question.

  • Conventional agent models take behaviour as an input: the designer writes the rule — when threatened → flee, this agent is aggressive.
  • You can then only ever "discover" what you already wrote. The model confirms your assumptions by construction.
  • That is fatal for a developmental question. To learn how a disposition forms, you cannot begin by coding the disposition in.
  • Why do two people with similar starts diverge? How does a temperament become a character? Those are questions a rule-based model cannot honestly produce.
So the whole design had to be inverted.
PsychSim · the inversion
02 The one idea

Behaviour is the output.

  • PsychSim specifies only the neural machinery — circuits, their wiring, and the chemistry that makes connections excite or inhibit.
  • That machinery has no concept of behaviour. It knows only how to pass activation between circuits.
  • What an agent does is whatever the machinery produces on meeting a situation — after a lifetime of development has shaped it.
  • A result is a genuine finding about the mechanism, checkable against real data — not a restatement of an assumption.

03 What it is

A general platform for how personality develops.

  • It provides the brain, the world, the developmental machinery, and the measurement tools — not a model of one condition.
  • Specific questions attach as modules / instruments on top of the shared, validated substrate. The value is the platform and its extensibility.
  • The driving subject: how a developing non-offending psychopath — a "sophropath", the callous-unemotional pathway — forms. But the platform grows a life and reads what emerges.
  • Descriptive, no verdict. It produces a life; the observer describes what that life became. It never judges and never labels the agent.

04 The substrate

A category-free brain.

  • ~82 nucleus-level circuits joined by ~206 receptor-signed pathways — the regions the literature ties to threat, reward, social perception, interoception, and executive control.
  • No drives, no built-in good/bad. This replaced an earlier Panksepp 7-drive engine, deliberately retired — an "honesty cut".
  • Experience enters only through 9 sensory channels (incl. IN-CONSPEC, an "another individual" channel). The world can present a stimulus — it can never set a circuit.
  • The whole brain is one reviewed data file — a "seed", refined across 13 documented versions. Revising the model edits data, not code.

05 Signs from chemistry

The sign is derived, never chosen.

  • Whether a pathway excites or inhibits is set by the transmitter and the target receptor, taken from the literature — not by the behaviour the designer wants.
  • Flipping a sign would mean falsifying a citation — a visible lie, not a quiet tweak.
  • Inhibition is kept because it exists, even when it works against a study's phenomenon. Keeping only the edges that drive your outcome is coding the outcome.
  • Serotonin forced the rule: its sign is opposite across targets — it dampens the attack area (5-HT1A) while facilitating the prefrontal controllers (5-HT2A).
Lower 5-HT tone → more provoked aggression — the real clinical direction — emerging from the net of competing signed pathways. No coded "5-HT → less aggression" rule.

06 Living a life

Four matrices present situations; the substrate responds.

  • A brain in a vat develops nothing. The substrate grows inside a world of four matrices — social (relationships), environmental (places & things), group (standing & belonging), self-reflection (its developing model of itself).
  • A matrix never says how to feel. It presents a situation — a pattern of stimulation — and the substrate produces the response.
  • A relationship is not a stored number; it is a history of encounters, each lived through the substrate. The bond is whatever those encounters built.
  • The loop closes: experience shapes the substrate → the substrate produces behaviour → behaviour changes the bond, the standing, the self → that becomes the next situation.
The mundane counts: a rare sharp episode and the ordinary walk to school, repeated for years, both shape who the agent becomes.

07 Real developmental time

Two identical newborns become two different people.

  • Every agent starts as a newborn: the same generic substrate, a handful of cited innate biases (sweet taste rewards; a looming shape threatens). Everything else is learned.
  • Learning is plasticity — strong early, tapering with age on a realistic schedule. Childhood lays down the adult's stable temperament. When matters as much as what.
  • Real time, never compressed. A 60-year life is a genuinely long run — a full substrate settle per person, per act.
  • Identical starting brains, raised differently, diverge — not because the difference was coded, but because they lived different lives through the same machinery.

08 The honesty wall · the thesis

The mechanism must never contain the answer.

Any outcome under study — and anything that would trivially produce it — is never written into the machinery. Outcomes are always downstream: measured, emergent, discovered. And it's enforced structurally, so the dishonest move is impossible, not discouraged.

09 The observer

Measuring without interfering.

  • To study an agent you must measure it — but measurement must never change what it measures, or the finding is contaminated.
  • Strict separation of substrate and observer. The observer reads behaviour and computes the constructs a researcher cares about — empathy, impulsivity, executive control, a CU signature — one-way.
  • The constructs live in the observer's report, not the agent's brain. If "empathy" were a dial inside the agent, the model would assume the very thing it should discover.
  • A temperament seed (e.g. fearless — a low-fear starting disposition) is a legitimate input; the CU outcome is only ever a measured read-out. Naming an input after an outcome pre-supposes the answer.
The report describes; it never prescribes. The names live in the observer, not in the brain.

10 Emergence · the credibility highlight

A known phenomenon fell out of a mechanism never built to produce it.

  • A continuous-maturation mechanism was added for an unrelated reason — executive control keeps maturing into the mid-20s while reward-sensitivity peaks in mid-adolescence. Age enters only as a rate; no "risk" rule anywhere.
  • What emerged: an adolescent risk-taking inverted-U, peaking in adolescence, above both childhood and adulthood — matching the developmental literature.
  • It emerged rather than being fitted. Nothing was tuned to produce it. That is the difference between confirming an assumption and discovering a pattern.

11 A worked example

Proving the answer wasn't smuggled in.

Take aggression. The naive move: make the attack area VMHvl fire strongly. PsychSim instead wires it as the anatomy dictates — provocation reaches it; a prefrontal brake and the maturing executive can hold it below threshold — then measures the outcome.

Bonus: early on, strong provocation shows overt aggression; by age ~8 the same provocation is held below threshold by the maturing brake — reactive aggression's real developmental course, un-tuned.

12 The instruments

Town, Arena, scan controller, bank.

the town

the population view

many agents in a generated settlement — households, school, work, ONS-anchored demography. Coarse per-agent detail, slow.

the arena · the fine-detail lens

a few agents, high resolution

2–5 agents in a confined micro-environment, developed & watched at depth. Confinement is structural — few things present — never a "stressful" label.

the scan controller

the discovery engine

searches which configurations produce which measured outcomes. Cannot be handed a target; every hit is a candidate hypothesis.

the bank

grow once, reuse

save a costly developed life-course; re-hydrate it for many studies — restored, never edited.

On its very first run, the Arena caught a silent plumbing null that would have produced a fake "agents don't engage" finding — exactly what a regression harness exists to catch.

14 Why it matters

Findings worth trusting — because the structure removes the chance to cheat.

  • A biologically-grounded platform in which behaviour emerges rather than being coded — a validated instrument and a defined method.
  • Its credibility comes from enforced honesty, not careful designers. A result survives contact with people who don't know why the discipline matters.
  • It turns hard developmental questions — what experience, on what starting substrate, produces what adult? — into things you can discover, then test against the real world.
  • General and extensible: one platform, many studies, each inheriting the same guarantees because they are properties of the shared substrate.
