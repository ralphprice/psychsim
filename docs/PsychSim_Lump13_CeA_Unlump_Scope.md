# Lump #13 — CeA un-lumping. **SCOPE. The diagnosis found the decisive fact: a 2-way split is NOT enough.**

**The diagnosis caught exactly the thing that would have sunk a naive un-lumping, and I verified it: both
coupled outputs (`CeA → vlPAG-GABA` disinhibiting freezing, `CeA → HYPdm` suppressing aggression) leave from
what becomes CEm. So splitting CeA in two moves the coupling INTACT from CeA to CEm — one CEm activation would
still drive both opposite-signed outputs, and the exit measurement would still fail. This determines the whole
scope: the pass is not "split CeA in two," it is "split CeA into a SELECTOR and DIFFERENTIABLE OUTPUT
POPULATIONS, wired so the selector gates the freezing and aggression outputs SEPARATELY." Here is the staged
scope.**

---

## 1. What the diagnosis established (verified)

- **The afferent routing is grounded and clean:** CEl takes all 17 external afferents (sensory/cortical/
  thalamic/intra-amygdalar/neuromodulatory); CEm takes ZERO external afferents (driven from CEl + ITC) —
  exactly as the anatomy predicts. Two are grounded harder and land by name: `PVN-OT → CEl` (already grounded
  in the seed's own CeA-GABA function string, Knobloch 2012, a CeL finding) and `PBN → CEl` (the canonical
  PBN→CeL nociceptive route). `ITC` splits into `ITC→CEl` + `ITC→CEm` (the literature has ITC inhibiting both —
  one edge becoming two, not moving).
- **All 6 outputs leave from CEm** (vlPAG-GABA, HYPdm, BNST, PVN, RVLM, LC) — hypothalamic/PAG/brainstem
  defensive-column outputs, none intra-amygdalar. Clean.
- **Two of the three internal pieces already exist:** `CeA-GABA` is already named "CeL GABAergic interneuron
  population" and was built as the CeL→CeM feed-forward inhibition (just gets its endpoints named); the Stage-2
  `CeA → CeA-GABA` loop becomes the drive recruiting the CEl inhibitory population (survives intact).
- **★ The decisive finding, verified:** both coupled outputs leave from CEm. **A 2-way split relocates the
  coupling to CEm; it does not resolve it.** The resolution requires the CEm outputs to be DIFFERENTIABLE and
  the CEl selector to gate them SEPARATELY.

## 2. The scope — three stages, because this is the largest connectome-restructuring pass in the project

**Stage A alone (the anatomical split) is necessary but NOT sufficient — it moves the coupling to CEm. Stages B
and C are what resolve it. Stage them, measure between, ground every one of the ~24 moving edges.**

### STAGE A — the anatomical split (the grounded re-routing the diagnosis mapped)
- Create **CEl** (lateral) and **CEm** (medial) as distinct nodes; retire the single `CeA` lump into them.
- **CEl takes all 17 external afferents** (grounded per the diagnosis — sensory/cortical/thalamic/intra-
  amygdalar/neuromodulatory in). **CEm takes zero external afferents.**
- **`ITC` splits** into `ITC → CEl` and `ITC → CEm` (grounded — ITC inhibits both divisions).
- **`CeA-GABA` maps onto the CEl→CEm feed-forward inhibition** (already named as CeL; just name its
  endpoints). The **Stage-2 `CEl → CEl-GABA` loop survives** (the drive recruiting the CEl inhibitory
  population).
- **All 6 outputs leave from CEm** (re-pointed from the lump to CEm).
- **Measure after Stage A:** where does the drive concentrate? Is the coupling now on CEm (expected)? **And the
  ceiling: does CEm's output convergence still saturate, or did splitting the 15-afferent load onto CEl relieve
  it?** (Treat the ceiling relief as a PREDICTION TO MEASURE, not an assumed benefit — CEl's load is relieved,
  but CEm's convergence may not be; where the 1.0 saturation lands after the split is an empirical question.)

### STAGE B — differentiate the CEm output populations (grounded in CeM output heterogeneity)
- **Real CeM has heterogeneous output neurons targeting PAG vs hypothalamus differentially.** Split CEm's
  output stage into at least two differentiable populations: **a freezing-driving output population** (→
  `vlPAG-GABA`, the disinhibition of the freezing column) and **an aggression/defensive output population** (→
  `HYPdm`, whose suppression must be liftable independently). The other outputs (BNST, PVN, RVLM, LC) route to
  whichever population the anatomy assigns (autonomic/arousal outputs likely track the defensive population;
  ground each).
- **This is the grounding that makes the coupling resolvable:** if the freezing-output and aggression-output
  populations are distinct, they can be in DIFFERENT activation states — which a single lumped CEm cannot.
- **Measure after Stage B:** are the two output populations independently addressable? (Not yet resolved —
  they need the selector to CHOOSE between them, Stage C — but they must first be distinct.)

### STAGE C — the CEl SOM+/PKCδ+ mutual-inhibition selector, gating the CEm populations separately
- **Add the winner-take-all selector in CEl** (the one genuinely NEW element): the SOM+ ⊣ PKCδ+ mutual
  inhibition, grounded in the CeA selection-microcircuit literature (the SOM+ population inhibits the PKCδ+
  population; both gate CEm output).
- **Wire the selector to gate the two CEm output populations SEPARATELY** — so that when the selector settles
  on one mode (e.g. freezing), it drives the freezing-output population AND suppresses the aggression-output
  population, and vice versa. **This is what makes freezing and aggression DIFFERENT CeA output states rather
  than one activation.**
- **The exit measurement — the coupling resolution (this closes S56):** in a freezing-appropriate context, does
  the freezing column fire (CEm freezing-output active) WITHOUT the aggression output being suppressed as a
  side effect? In an aggression-appropriate context (provocation), does the aggression output recruit HYPdm/
  dPAG (aggression-output population active, freezing-output not) — **without the two trading against each
  other?** If freezing and aggression are now drivable as distinct selector states, **the coupling is resolved
  and S56 is complete.**

## 3. What the exit measurement unblocks

**This is the final step of S56, and its completion unblocks the harsh mirror — the demonstration that began
this entire descent.** Once CeA selects rather than saturates, the aggression output can be driven in
provocation contexts, which means the wary → defensive developmental direction becomes producible: a harsh
cohort/environment can now strain relationships (aggressive acts become expressible), forming wary
relationships that develop the defensive mirror of the warm → reward result. **The harsh mirror — owed for the
study's even-handedness since the F1–F4 pass — becomes available the moment the coupling resolves.**

## 4. The discipline for this pass (it is connectome surgery)

- **Ground every one of the ~24 moving edges.** The diagnosis mapped them; the build must verify each lands
  where the anatomy says (CEl for afferents, CEm for outputs, the two ITC edges, the internal selector), not
  where convenience suggests. **Any edge whose grounding is uncertain gets flagged, not assumed.**
- **Stage and measure between A, B, C.** Stage A relocates the coupling (measure it's on CEm); Stage B makes
  the outputs distinct (measure they're addressable); Stage C adds the selector (measure the coupling
  resolves). **Do not build all three and measure once — the staging is how you know which stage does what, and
  the branch has repeatedly shown that fixing one masked defect reveals the next.**
- **The ceiling is a prediction, not a benefit.** Measure whether CEm's convergence saturates after the split;
  if it does, that is a finding (the ceiling limitation partly survives the un-lumping), not a failure.
- **Regrow at each stage** (nodes and edges change). **Gate on the full suite.** **No classification should
  flip** from an anatomical re-routing — verify.
- **The selector and the differentiated outputs must produce SELECTION emergently** — the selector chooses
  based on the substrate's own drives, never a scripted "if provocation then aggression." The honesty wall
  holds: we wire the selection MICROCIRCUIT; which mode wins emerges from the drives.
