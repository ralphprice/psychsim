**This is the grounded resolution, and it is not merely convenient — it is how the biology works:**

> **Inhibitory synaptic plasticity actively maintains the E-I balance at a set-point.** *"Plasticity at
> inhibitory synapses maintains balanced excitatory and inhibitory synaptic inputs at cortical neurons"*

> strikingly constant ratio of E/I synapse numbers."* E-I balance is *"established during early postnatal
> development"* AND *"must also be dynamically maintained throughout life"* — it is **not** left to erode.
> **And critically for the load-scaling: "strong recurrent excitation required STRONGER inhibitory-excitatory
> feedback to enforce" the stable fixed point** (the computational-model result) — with nonplastic inhibition,
> "if the excitatory feedback became strong enough, the stationary firing rates lost stability."

> **★ This says exactly what the model needs and exactly what it lacks: the inhibitory return limb should be
