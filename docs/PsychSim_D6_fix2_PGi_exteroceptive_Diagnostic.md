# D6 #2 door 1 — `PGi`'s exteroceptive arm: diagnostic (NOTHING built; the convenient answer refused)

The spec's flag was right, and following it changed the answer. **`SC-Pv → PGi` is not built, and the reason
is not that it's ungrounded — it's that the honest question has a different, less convenient answer.**

## 1. The model fact (verifiable): observed distress reaches LC ONLY through the amygdala
Traced every route `IN-VIS:biological_motion → LC` (≤6 hops):
- **Exactly ONE route exists:** `IN-VIS:biological_motion → SC-Pv → CeA → LC`.
- **ZERO non-amygdala routes.** `SC-Pv`'s only efferents are `CeA` and `dPAG` — it does not reach `PGi` or `LC`.

So the vicarious entailment is not "CeA happens to be in the path." **For visual/social distress, the amygdala
is the *only* path to the arousal/teaching signal.**

## 2. The question asked (not "what could reach PGi", but "what IS PGi's afferent set")
The spec named the paper: **Van Bockstaele, Akaoka & Aston-Jones 1993 — "Brainstem afferents to the rostral
(juxtafacial) nucleus paragigantocellularis."** The candidate `SC-Pv → PGi` scores four-for-four (already built,
already afferented — passes S35 — carries `biological_motion`, would dissolve the entailment in exactly the
right context) — **and its fifth property is that its grounding is a FUNCTION description** ("PGi integrates
exteroceptive input"), **not a cited projection.** S35-passing is a reason to check it carefully, not to accept
it.

**⚠️ Access limitation, stated plainly:** I cannot fetch Van Bockstaele 1993 (no external access this session),
so I cannot read its afferent list directly. What follows distinguishes what I can establish from what I infer.

**What the title and the field establish:** the paper documents **brainstem** afferents; **`SC-Pv` is midbrain
tectum.** PGi's *exteroceptive* arm, as the integrator that relays somatic/pain signals to LC, is
**somatosensory** — spinal dorsal horn (lamina I/V nociceptive/somatosensory) and trigeminal — i.e.
**exteroception of the body surface, not teloreception (vision/audition).** The visceral arm is `NTS`/`PBN`.
**I am not aware of a documented VISUAL/collicular projection to the LC-projecting PGi**, and the paper's own
framing (brainstem afferents, ventral tegmentum) does not obviously include one. *This is inference from the
field, flagged as such — it needs the paper's actual afferent list to settle.*

## 3. ★ The consequence — the vicarious entailment may be a FINDING, not an artifact
The direct-pain entailment **was** an artifact of an incomplete hub: `CeA` carried 100% only because `PGi` was
absent, and `PGi`'s nociceptive afferent gave pain a non-amygdala route (72%→37%). **The vicarious case is
different in kind:**

- If `PGi`'s exteroceptive arm is **somatosensory** (spinal/trigeminal), completing it does **nothing** for
  vicarious distress, which is **visual/auditory**. `PGi` is the wrong integrator for social distress.
- And there is **no other** non-amygdala route in the anatomy (or the model). Visual salience → `SC` → amygdala
  → arousal **is** how observed distress engages the arousal system.

**So "low affective empathy impairs vicarious learning" may be TRUE BY ANATOMY, not TRUE BY CONSTRUCTION** —
a *real dependency*, not a methodological artifact. The distinction is exact:
- **artifact** (direct pain): `CeA` was in the path only because the real route (`PGi`) was missing → dissolved
  by building it.
- **finding** (vicarious): `CeA` is in the path because observed distress **must be affectively appraised** to
  reach arousal — there is no visual→arousal route that bypasses the amygdala. Completing `PGi` cannot dissolve
  it, because `PGi` doesn't carry vision.

**If that holds, the D6 #2 verdict FLIPS for the vicarious context: from `ENTAILED` (the experiment cannot
answer) to a real mechanism the experiment DOES reveal** — observed distress reaches the teaching signal only
through the affective appraisal, so a blunted appraisal genuinely reduces vicarious learning. The CU study
could then make the claim *because it is true*, not despite it being true by construction.

## 4. Why I refuse `SC-Pv → PGi` — "no result is a target, both ways"
Routing `SC-Pv → PGi` would dissolve the entailment and close door 1 under vicarious distress — **the wanted
result.** But it is grounded on a functional inference, it points a midbrain tectal source at a nucleus whose
cited afferents are brainstem, and **the answer it gives is the convenient one.** Building it would be accepting
a wanted-looking result an artifact (a mis-grounded edge) might produce — the exact error, in the exact place
the spec warned it would be most tempting. **Not built.**

## 5. What resolves this — and it is not mine to guess
Two outcomes, and the paper decides between them:

  LC-projecting PGi.** Then build *that* (whatever it actually is), re-measure, and the vicarious entailment
  may dissolve — the best outcome, honestly earned.
- **(b) It does not** (PGi's exteroceptive arm is somatosensory only). Then the vicarious entailment is a
  **real dependency**, door 1 does **not** close by completing `PGi`, and the honest move is to **reframe the
  D6 #2 vicarious verdict from `ENTAILED` to a characterised finding** — *and stop trying to dissolve it.*
